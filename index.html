Plant Information


Plant Name: Kaner


Benefits: Kaner, or Nerium oleander, is well-regarded in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties, although it must be used cautiously due to its toxicity. It contains compounds with antibacterial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties. Some herbal preparations utilize Kaner for treating skin conditions, joint pain, and respiratory ailments. In small, controlled amounts, its extracts are sometimes used in Ayurvedic medicine for their potential to alleviate digestive issues and relieve inflammation. However, any use for medicinal purposes should be under expert supervision due to its potent effects.



Season: The Kaner plant typically thrives in warm climates and is known for its year-round green foliage. However, it blooms most vibrantly during the summer months, from late spring to early fall, with beautiful clusters of flowers in shades of white, pink, or red. These flowers are highly fragrant and can attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. In warm, frost-free regions, Kaner may continue to flower sporadically throughout the year, though peak blooms are seen in summer.



Region: Native to the Mediterranean region, Kaner is now found in tropical and subtropical areas worldwide, including South Asia, Australia, and parts of North America. It adapts well to warm and dry climates, thriving in regions with well-drained soils and ample sunlight. In India, it is a common sight along roadsides, gardens, and parks due to its resilience and attractive blooms. Its hardiness and ability to tolerate drought make it popular in regions with arid climates, while it’s also cultivated as an ornamental plant in diverse settings.



Age: The Kaner plant is a perennial shrub and can live for several decades, with some plants reaching 20-30 years under favorable conditions. It has a slow growth rate, especially when grown from seeds, but it can reach a height of 6-12 feet with proper care and conditions. Regular pruning helps maintain its shape and encourage denser foliage, enhancing its ornamental value. Kaner plants grown in less favorable conditions may show stunted growth, but they remain resilient, often adapting well to their surroundings.
